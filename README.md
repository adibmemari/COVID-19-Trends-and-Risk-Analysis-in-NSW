# COVID-19-Trends-and-Risk-Analysis-in-NSW
Analyzing how COVID-19 has spread across different age groups, regions, and sources of infection provides valuable insights that can assist public health professionals in making informed decisions regarding the impacts of the virus

The analysis shows a disproportionately higher mortality rate among individuals aged 70+, indicating increased vulnerability in elderly populations. This highlights the importance of targeted public health interventions such as vaccination prioritisation. I conducted exploratory data analysis on NSW COVID-19 datasets using Python (Pandas, Matplotlib) to identify trends across age groups, regions, and infection sources. Discovered higher mortality risk among elderly populations and variations in transmission sources, supporting data-driven public health insights.

Overall the NSW COVID-19 data analysis project demonstrates that while the virus spread broadly across multiple regions and age groups, driven largely by community transmission, its impact was uneven across the population. The findings indicate that younger populations contributed significantly to overall case numbers, whereas severe outcomes were disproportionately concentrated among older individuals, highlighting clear differences between transmission patterns and health risk. Regional variations suggest that certain areas experienced higher case loads, potentially influenced by factors such as population density and mobility. Additionally, the presence of locally acquired and unknown infection sources points to sustained community spread and possible limitations in contact tracing efforts. Overall, the project reveals that COVID-19 is not only a widespread public health issue but also one that affects groups differently, emphasising the importance of targeted interventions such as prioritising vulnerable populations, strengthening surveillance systems, and focusing resources on high-risk regions to improve public health outcomes.
